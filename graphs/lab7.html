Matrices in Sage

Here we'll be working with matrices in Sage. Some points to keep in mind are:
  • You can multiply matrices in Sage like numbers, e.g., A*B or T*v. You can also exponentiate via A**10
  • You can generate column vectors with the column_matrix command, e.g., column_matrix([1,2,3])
  • You can access Sage matrix rows and elements just like when we worked with matrices in Python, e.g., A[i] for the i-th row or A[i][j] for the (i,j) entry.
  • However, you cannot easily modify individual Sage matrix entries. Consequently, if you want to modify a matrix, it is often easiest to make a new one. For instance, to make a transition matrix T from A, it's probably easiest to build T as a list of lists, as we made our matrices in Python, then convert it to a Sage matrix with the matrix command. This is what I did for my tm function.
  • If you want to format some of your results differently, you can use my matprint to print matrices with less precision. You can also transform a column vector v to a row vector with v.transpose().
